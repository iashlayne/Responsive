My Story

Hello, my name is Iashlayne Kane Medina. I’m an advanced scuba diver who started diving at the age of 18. I never knew it would become my favorite hobby.

In the past, I used to be afraid of water. I found the idea of being in the deep sea terrifying. However, when I overcame my fears, I realized that being in the ocean was like an escape from reality. Seeing the sea creatures dive with me was such a relaxing experience. It felt like I was one of them.

I encourage you to become a diver not only for fun but also to help save the sea world. Let’s explore the depths and have a good time.

What is Scuba Diving?

Self Contained Underwater Breathing Apparatus is referred to as SCUBA. People can explore the undersea world without holding their breath by participating in scuba diving. One of the closest experiences to Zero G is undoubtedly floating weightlessly in mid-water.

Skills of Diver Should Know

Beginning with online lessons and a knowledge test, the "Open Water" learn-to-scuba course offered by this PADI organization covers all the fundamentals you'll need to know to become a safe novice diver. You must be able to swim without a mask for a brief period of time in order to do some of these talents. Try snorkeling without a mask on if you'd want to get some practice before enrolling in a scuba class. Speak with your diving instructor; they ought to be able to assist you in achieving each of these objectives.

You always have to dive with your buddy, if you get lost try to find your buddy. If you can’t find him, you need to ascend accordingly to your depth.

Standard Beginner's Equipment Lineup

  • • Mask and snorkel
  • • Weights and buoyancy control device (BCD)
  • • A regulator with a first, second stage, and octopus
  • • Depth gauge
  • • Submersible Pressure gauge (SPG)
  • • Scuba diving tank
  • • Diving Fins
